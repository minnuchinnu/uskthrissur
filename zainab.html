spice up your sketch

-Zainav Tambawalla

- Nationality Egypt resident in Saudi Arabia

-spice up your sketch

Workshop Description

we know drawing and painting such a crowded and complex scene like old buildings or cluttered shops facades specially in old areas can be very challenging, sometimes itâ€™s even overwhelming , with all the complex architectural elements that must be drawn correctly in perspective and right tones of shades, specially in place full of live like India there are many strategies in simplifying a complex scene without being overwhelmed. In this workshop we will focus on shades and shadows and how we can use them in an expressive and loose way to simplifying the scene and create depth but also solve the interlocking shapes that any scene consists of !

Supply List

-waterproof ink pens (technical or fountain pens or nip pen)

-Watercolour paints (I prefer tubes but if you like watercolour pans bring a plastic palette with you to mix the shadow colours

-Sketchbook or loose paper, preferably watercolour paper or water-friendly paper at lest A4 size or bigger

-Brushes (try to have at least one big brush size 8 or larger and a small one for details also I highly recommend adding a flat brush)

-plastic container of water

-A stool to sit on while working (optional)
